How Ranking Works

The score is a recommendation signal, not a judgment of paper quality. If something looks wrong, use Save or Not Interested and the ranking will adapt.

Papers rise to the top when they align with more of the signals you care about:

  • They match authors, labs, or topics from your Research Setup.
  • They are recent, unless your freshness settings prefer older work too.
  • They are highly cited.
  • You have previously saved similar papers.
  • AI scoring is enabled and rates the paper as relevant.

Signals stack. A paper by a tracked author at a tracked lab on a tracked topic will usually rank much higher than a paper matching only one weak signal.

Ranking Explanations

Each paper shows why it was recommended. Expand the score details when you want to understand whether a result came from an author match, a topic match, citation strength, or saved-paper similarity.

  • “Matched author: Kaiming He”

Enrichment

Enrichment means the app adds extra metadata after a paper is ingested, so you can sort, filter, and inspect papers with more context.

Citations

Citation counts from Semantic Scholar. Sort by Most Cited when you want influential work first instead of the newest work.

OpenAlex

Topic classifications, open-access status, and additional citation data from OpenAlex. Enabled by default so papers arrive with better metadata.

SPECTER2 Embeddings

Scientific paper embeddings power semantic search and related-paper recommendations. This is what lets the app find papers by meaning, not just exact words.

PDF Thumbnails

First-page thumbnails for quick visual scanning. Helpful when you want to skim many papers faster.

Corpus Analytics

Use these tools when you want to explore your whole library at a higher level instead of reviewing one paper at a time.

Topic Clusters

Groups recent papers by theme so you can see which areas of your feed are active.

Emerging Topics

Compares recent trends against a baseline to spot research directions that are starting to pick up.

Neighbor Papers

Finds additional papers similar to a collection or a set of seed papers you already like.

Related Papers & Duplicates

Related Papers

Expand a paper’s details to see similar papers already in your library. This is useful when you want alternative baselines, follow-up work, or adjacent methods.

Duplicate Detection

Papers with nearly identical titles get an orange Dup badge so you can avoid reviewing or exporting the same work twice.
