How Ranking Works

You don’t need to understand the formula. If something looks wrong, use Save or Not Interested and the ranking will adjust.

Papers rise to the top when:

  • They match more of the authors, labs, or topics you entered.
  • They are newer.
  • They are highly cited.
  • You have previously saved similar papers.
  • AI scoring is enabled and rates the paper as relevant.

Multiple signals stack — a paper by a tracked author at a tracked lab on a tracked topic gets the highest score.

Ranking Explanations

Each paper shows why it was recommended. Expand the score details to see reasons like:

  • “Matched author: Kaiming He”
  • “Highly cited (342 citations)”
  • “Similar to saved: NeRF in the Wild”

Enrichment

Papers are automatically enriched with external data during scrapes.

Citations

Citation counts from Semantic Scholar. Papers show a blue citation badge you can click. Sort by Most Cited to find influential work.

OpenAlex

Topic classifications, open-access status, and additional citation data from OpenAlex. Enabled by default.

SPECTER2 Embeddings

Scientific paper embeddings power semantic search and related-paper recommendations. The model (~420MB) downloads automatically on first use.

PDF Thumbnails

First-page thumbnails for visual scanning. Generated during scrapes.

Corpus Analytics

Explore your paper library at a higher level.

Topic Clusters

Groups recent papers by theme so you can see what areas are active.

Emerging Topics

Compares recent trends against a baseline to spot new research directions.

Neighbor Papers

Finds undiscovered papers similar to a collection or set of seed papers.

Related Papers & Duplicates

Related Papers

Expand a paper’s details to see links to similar papers from your library. Uses SPECTER2 embeddings for high-quality semantic matching.

Duplicate Detection

Papers with nearly identical titles get an orange Dup badge so you know at a glance.
