Quick Start

The core loop is simple: tell the app what you care about, fetch new papers, and triage the results.

Note: Start simple

You do not need Gmail, AI, export tools, or automation before the app becomes useful. Get the basic workflow working first.

1

Add interests

Add authors, labs, or short topic phrases in Settings → Research Setup. Start with a short, focused list instead of every possible keyword.

2

Run Scrape

Click Run Scrape in the top navigation. The app pulls recent papers from your enabled feeds, scores them against your interests, and adds the best matches to your inbox.

3

Review Inbox

On the Papers page, use Save for papers you want to keep and Not Interested for weak matches. Those actions also improve future ranking.

Your Normal Workflow

Step 1

Tell it what you care about

In Research Setup, enter one item per line. Good starting inputs are researcher names, lab names, and topic phrases you would actually type into a search box.

Step 2

Run a scrape

Press Run Scrape. The app fetches new papers, enriches them with metadata, ranks them, and builds your inbox with the results worth reviewing.

Tip: One scrape per day is usually enough unless you are backfilling older papers or testing new settings.

Step 3

Review the inbox

Open the Papers page. Save the papers you want to keep, and mark weak matches as Not Interested. This feedback helps the ranking adapt to your taste over time.

Step 4

Come back to Saved

The Saved tab is your shortlist. Use it for papers you want to revisit, cite, export, or send to Mendeley/Zotero later.

If The Results Look Off

Too few papers

Broaden your topic phrases, add a few labs, or remove terms that are too niche. The app needs enough signal to find related work.

Too much noise

Remove generic keywords, use Not Interested aggressively, and add recurring false positives to muted topics or muted authors.
