Search

Use the dashboard search bar to search papers that are already in your library. If you want to pull in older arXiv papers you do not have yet, use Discover instead.

Keyword

Best when you know the exact term you want: an author name, a dataset, a method name, or a phrase that should appear literally.

Semantic

Best when the wording may vary. It searches by meaning, so “image classification” can still match papers phrased as “visual recognition”.

Hybrid

Combines exact-word matches with semantic matches. This is the default because it is usually the safest choice when you are not sure.

Use keyword when wording matters. Use semantic when ideas matter. Use hybrid when you want both.

Filters & Sorting

Match type filter

Show only Author, Affiliation, or Title matches. This is useful when you want to understand why papers are appearing.

Advanced Filters

Filter by arXiv category, resource availability, reading status, or include hidden papers when you want to audit past decisions.

Sort options

Sort by score for your best matches, by date for recency, or by citation count when you want influential work first.

Time windows

Switch between daily, weekly, monthly, or all-time views to narrow the review queue.

Saved Searches

Save a filter combination so you can get back to the same slice of papers with one click.

Creating

Set up your filters, then click Save search. Name it after the question it answers, such as “recent diffusion papers” or “high-citation 3D vision”.

Using

Saved searches appear as quick-access pills on the dashboard. Click one to instantly restore its filters and sorting.

Discover (Historical Search)

Use the Discover page when you want to search older arXiv papers by date range and category. It backfills historical papers into your library, and any matches are added to your inbox like a normal scrape.
