UI Guide

The Papers view is where you spend most of your time. Think of it as a triage queue: review new matches in the inbox, then keep the important ones in Saved.

TL;DR summaries: Expand a paper with More details (or press d) to read its short summary. Without LLM enabled, this is extracted from the abstract. With LLM enabled, it becomes an AI-generated plain-language description. Adjust the number of visible lines in Settings → Ranking → Summary lines (default: 3).

Layout

Left sidebar

Inbox and Saved (with counts), your collections and saved searches, and the filter groups (match type, venue, category, resources, reading status).

Top bar

Search, sort, the Visual grid / List view density toggle, Select for bulk actions, and the Run Scrape button.

Paper list

A dense, keyboard-friendly triage queue by default. Switch to Visual grid to browse by first-page teaser figures instead.

Most day-to-day use is just this: run a scrape, review Inbox, and keep your shortlist in Saved.

Paper Actions

Save

Keeps the paper in your shortlist and tells the ranking system to show you more like it.

Not Interested

Hides the paper and teaches the app to show fewer papers with similar signals.

Priority

Marks a must-read. Use it sparingly for the papers you want to surface above normal saves.

BibTeX

Copies one citation entry. The sidebar Export group exports all currently visible papers as BibTeX or an HTML report.

Select

Enters bulk mode so you can save, skip, or export multiple papers at once.

More details

Expands the abstract, notes, related papers, follow/mute controls, and other metadata for that paper.

Rule of thumb: if you ignore every other control, Save and Not Interested are enough to make the app useful.
