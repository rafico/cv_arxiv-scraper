UI Guide

The Papers view is where you spend most of your time. Think of it as a triage queue: review new matches in the inbox, then keep the important ones in Saved.

Navigation

Run Scrape

Fetches recent papers from your enabled feeds and refreshes the inbox with anything worth reviewing.

Inbox

New matches that still need a decision. This is your daily review queue.

Saved

Your keep list. Use it for papers you may cite, share, read later, or export.

Most day-to-day use is just this: run a scrape, review Inbox, and keep your shortlist in Saved.

Paper Actions

Save

Keeps the paper in your shortlist and tells the ranking system to show you more like it.

Not Interested

Hides the paper and teaches the app to show fewer papers with similar signals.

Priority

Marks a must-read. Use it sparingly for the papers you want to surface above normal saves.

BibTeX

Copies one citation entry. The toolbar BibTeX button exports all currently visible papers.

Select

Enters bulk mode so you can save, skip, or export multiple papers at once.

More details

Expands the abstract, notes, related papers, follow/mute controls, and other metadata for that paper.

Rule of thumb: if you ignore every other control, Save and Not Interested are enough to make the app useful.
