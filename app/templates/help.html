Help

A plain-English guide to finding useful computer vision papers without checking arXiv all day.

Start Here

If you are new, focus on the basics first. You can ignore Gmail and AI until the basics are working.

Quick Start
  1. 1. Add a few authors, labs, or topics in Settings → Research Setup.
  2. 2. Click Run Scrape to collect matching papers.
  3. 3. On the Papers page, use Save for good papers and Not Interested for bad matches.

What This App Does

It finds papers for you

The app checks the arXiv computer vision feed and pulls in papers that match the authors, labs, and topics you care about.

It stays on your machine

There is no signup and no separate cloud account. Your data stays local unless you choose to send yourself email digests.

Your Normal Workflow

Step 1

Tell it what you care about

In Research Setup, enter one item per line. Start small: a few authors, a few labs, and a few topic phrases.

Step 2

Run a scrape

Press Run Scrape. The app will fetch recent papers and build your inbox.

Step 3

Review the inbox

Open the Papers page. Save the papers you want to keep, and mark weak matches as Not Interested.

Step 4

Come back to Saved

The Saved tab is your shortlist. Use it for papers you want to revisit, cite, or share.

What The Main Buttons Mean

Run Scrape

Checks arXiv for recent papers and refreshes your inbox.

Inbox

Shows papers that matched your interests and still need review.

Saved

Your keep list. Use this for papers worth coming back to.

Save

Marks a paper as important and keeps it in your shortlist.

Upvote

Tells the app, "this is relevant." It helps similar papers move up.

Not Interested

Hides a weak match and teaches the app to show less of that kind of paper.

Search and filters

Use these when your list gets long. They help you narrow by topic, match type, timeframe, and more.

Export and BibTeX

Download your current paper list when you want to save or share it outside the app.

Paper Organization

Ways to keep your reading list tidy beyond Save and Not Interested.

Collections

Group papers into named folders (e.g. “Thesis Ch. 3”). Create collections, then use the folder icon on any paper card to file it. Filter the dashboard by collection using the pills at the top.

User Tags

Add custom tags to any paper with the + tag button. Tags appear as indigo chips and can be removed by clicking the × next to them.

Notes

Each paper has a free-text notes field (expand More details to see it). Notes auto-save as you type.

Reading Status

Mark papers as To Read, Reading, or Read using the dropdown on each card. You can filter by reading status in Advanced Filters.

Reference Manager Sync

Export or sync papers with external tools.

BibTeX

Click BibTeX on any paper card to copy a single entry. The toolbar BibTeX button exports all visible papers at once.

Mendeley

Connect your Mendeley account via OAuth in Settings → Automation. Once connected, use Sync Saved Papers to push your library.

Zotero

Enter your Zotero API key and user ID in Settings → Automation. You can sync to a specific Zotero collection if you like.

Smart Features

Features that run automatically behind the scenes.

Citation Counts

During each scrape, citation data is fetched from Semantic Scholar. Papers show a blue citation badge you can click to see the full Semantic Scholar page. You can also sort by Most Cited.

Follow Author / Mute Topic

Expand a paper’s details to find Follow and Mute buttons. Follow adds the first author to your tracked authors. Mute suppresses future papers with a matching topic tag.

Duplicate Detection

If a new paper has a nearly identical title to one already in the database, it gets an orange Dup badge so you know at a glance.

Historical Search

Search for papers in a specific date range using the historical search API. This lets you backfill your inbox with older papers you may have missed.

Scheduled Scrapes

Enable automatic daily scrapes by adding a scheduler section to your config.yaml. Once enabled, the app runs scrapes at your chosen time without needing cron.

Related Papers

Expand a paper’s details to see links to similar papers from your library. Similarity is computed from title and abstract text.

Power User Tips

Shortcuts and tools for moving through papers faster.

Keyboard Shortcuts

j/k to navigate between papers, s to save, u to upvote, x to skip, o to open on arXiv, p to open PDF, d to toggle details.

Advanced Filters

Click Advanced Filters below the search bar to filter by arXiv category, resource availability, reading status, or to show hidden papers.

Saved Searches

After setting up filters, click Save search to bookmark the combination. Saved searches appear as quick-access pills above the paper list.

Bulk Operations

Click Select in the toolbar to enter bulk mode. You can then select multiple papers and save, upvote, or skip them all at once.

Settings, Explained Simply

Research Setup

Start Here

This is the most important section. Add authors, labs, and topic keywords. One item per line is enough.

Ranking

Optional

Fine-tunes how strongly the app reacts to authors, labs, topics, freshness, and muted terms. Safe to ignore at first.

Automation

Optional

Use this only if you want email digests or external sync. The app works fine without it.

LLM / AI

Optional

Adds AI summaries and an extra relevance score. Useful, but not required to get value from the app.

How Ranking Works

You do not need to understand the scoring formula to use the app well. In plain English, papers usually rise to the top when:

  • • They match more of the authors, labs, or topics you entered.
  • • They are newer.
  • • You have previously saved or upvoted similar papers.
  • • AI scoring is enabled and the paper looks relevant.

If something looks out of place, do not worry about the math. Use Save, Upvote, or Not Interested and the ranking will adjust over time.

Common Questions

Do I need Gmail?

No. Gmail is only for sending yourself digests by email. You can use the app normally without setting it up.

Do I need the AI features?

No. They are optional. The app can still match and rank papers using your authors, labs, and topics.

Why is my inbox empty?

Usually this means your tracked terms are very narrow, or there were no matches in the selected timeframe. Try broader topics or switch to a wider timeframe.

What should I learn first if I am not technical?

Learn just three things: add interests, run a scrape, and review papers with Save or Not Interested. Everything else can wait.
